Vaishno Devi Temple

Vaishno Devi Temple, located in the Trikuta Mountains of Jammu and Kashmir, India, is one of the most revered Hindu pilgrimage sites. Dedicated to Goddess Vaishno Devi, an incarnation of the Hindu Mother Goddess, it attracts millions of devotees each year. Pilgrims undertake a challenging 13.5-kilometer trek from the base camp at Katra to the sacred cave shrine, where they seek the goddess's blessings.

Kedarnath Temple

Kedarnath, located in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand, India, is one of the most revered pilgrimage sites for Hindus. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, it is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, which are considered the holiest Shiva temples. The temple is situated at an elevation of 3,583 meters (11,755 feet) and is surrounded by majestic snow-clad peaks.

Omkareshwar Temple

Omkareshwar, located on the Mandhata Island in the Narmada River in Madhya Pradesh, India, is a prominent Hindu pilgrimage site. The island is shaped like the sacred Hindu symbol 'Om,' enhancing its spiritual significance. Omkareshwar is home to one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, the most revered Shiva temples.
